## University of Chicago Sociology Graduate Student Council Bylaws (Draft)

### Article I. Name

The name of this organization shall be the University of Chicago Department of Sociology Graduate Student Council, hereinafter referred to as the "Sociology Graduate Student Council," "Graduate Student Council," or "Grad Council." The Grad Council is the official organizing body of graduate students in the Department of Sociology and is officially recognized by the Department. The responsibilities of leadership of the Grad Council are delegated to its Executive Board (the "Board"), as described in Article IV.

### Article II. Purpose

The Grad Council's purpose is to:

1. Increase transparency and communication between graduate students and faculty.
2. Advocate on behalf of sociology graduate students.
3. Expand departmental professionalization offerings.
4. Provide a social space for graduate students across cohorts.

### Article III. Membership

#### 3.1 Eligibility

Membership and participation are free from discrimination on the basis of race, religion, color, sex, age, national origin or ancestry, genetic information, marital status, parental status, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, disability, veteran status, or any other status protected by University policy or applicable law.

All University of Chicago PhD students enrolled in programs housed in the Department of Sociology, including students pursuing joint degrees with Sociology, are members of the Grad Council. Faculty, staff, postdoctoral scholars, and alumni may participate in Grad Council meetings and activities as guests or honorary participants; however, they may not vote or hold office.

#### 3.2 Voting Rights

Each active member of the Grad Council shall be entitled to one (1) vote on matters submitted to the Sociology graduate student body, including elections and any referendums or bylaw amendments, as provided in these Bylaws. For purposes of voting, an active member is a currently enrolled graduate student in the Department of Sociology.

#### 3.3 Membership Duration and Dues

There shall be no membership dues. Membership begins upon matriculation into a PhD program housed in the Department of Sociology and ends when a student graduates or otherwise ceases enrollment in the Department.

### Article IV. Board Members

#### 4.1 Composition

The Sociology Student Council Board is composed of six elected board members across three core positions: Co-Chairs, Finance and Operations, and Activities Chairs. The Council will also elect two student representatives to serve on the colloquium committee and four to five members of the Fall Institute planning committee. Each position will ideally have two members to ensure multiple people are trained on a position at a time.

#### 4.2 Term

Each representative will serve a term of one year from September of the elected year to the following September. Representatives may be elected for a position more than once, but cannot serve more than two consecutive terms in the same position.

Elections will occur in early June of each year, but the previous Board members' terms will not end until September. This ensures a three-month overlap for Board members to train new members into their positions ahead of the academic year.

#### 4.3 Board Member Duties

##### Co-Chairs

The primary goal of the Co-Chairs is to improve transparent communication and collaborative decision-making between graduate students and across graduate students and faculty. Co-Chair responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

1. Planning and running quarterly Town Halls or forums between students and the Department Chair and DGS.
2. Planning and running quarterly Grad Council Board Meetings.
3. Planning and running quarterly All-Student meetings.
4. Facilitating collaboration across roles within the Grad Council with other bodies such as workshops and the Fall Institute planning committee.
5. Serving as primary representatives of student interests in meetings with faculty.
6. Ensuring transparent communication with all sociology graduate students regarding future planning and active conversations with faculty and staff.
7. Maintaining storage and archives of council materials to ensure ongoing development of the council in the future.

##### Finance and Operations

The primary goal of the Finance and Operations team is to intentionally utilize all available graduate student funds toward student-run events and initiatives. Responsibilities include:

1. Tracking the Grad Council budget.
2. Serving as liaisons with the Dean of Students office regarding GSSAC funds and departmental administrators regarding internal funds.
3. Managing the election process.
4. Taking meeting minutes during board and all-student meetings.
5. Stocking the lounge snacks and supplies.
6. When needed, working to find external or supplementary sources of funding for sociology events.

##### Activities Chairs

The primary goal of the Activities Chairs is to plan at least one social and one professionalization event each quarter. Additional responsibilities include:

1. Communicating graduate events to all students in the department in a timely and frequent manner.
2. Submitting requests and receipts to the finance team in a timely manner.
3. Working to establish interdepartmental collaboration when useful.
4. Gauging student interest in different types of social and professionalization events.
5. Maintaining a log of ideas for professionalization and social events for future planning purposes.

#### 4.4 Transitioning Roles

To ensure continuity of the Grad Council from year to year, outgoing and incoming Board Members shall complete a transition process following annual elections and whenever a vacancy is filled. The outgoing Board Member is responsible for providing the incoming Board Member with the information, access, and materials needed to carry out the role. A transition meeting should be held within two (2) weeks of election results being announced (or within two weeks of an appointment), and all transfers described in this section should be completed no later than November 1.

Ownership and Permissions:
The Finance and Operations Chair(s), in consultation with the Co-Chairs, shall maintain a current inventory of Grad Council accounts, permissions, and access instructions. During transitions, administrative access should be transferred so that at least two current Board Members have administrator rights for each account whenever possible.

Website:
Transfer administrator access for the Grad Council website (and any associated services such as domain registration, hosting, analytics, and email forwarding) to the incoming Board Member(s) designated to manage the website. Ensure that at least two current Board Members (typically a Co-Chair and Finance and Operations) have administrator access.

WhatsApp:
Transfer WhatsApp (or equivalent messaging platform) administrator rights to the incoming Board Members. Update the group description, group rules, and pinned messages to reflect current officers and official channels (for example, the listserv).

Google Drive:
Transfer ownership and administrative permissions of the Grad Council Google Drive/Shared Drive to the incoming Finance and Operations Chair(s) and Co-Chairs. Ensure core folders (meeting agendas and minutes, elections, finances, events, templates, and communications) are organized, appropriately named, and permissioned.

Training:
Outgoing Board Members shall provide training to incoming Board Members on recurring responsibilities, relevant departmental and university policies, and routine workflows (for example, scheduling rooms, reimbursement procedures, budget management, communications cadence). When available, outgoing Board Members should provide a brief handoff document including an annual calendar, event checklists, templates, and key contacts.

Updating Listserv:
At the beginning of each academic year (and at least once per academic quarter thereafter), the Finance and Operations Chair(s) or designated communications officer will update the Grad Council listserv and any other official mailing lists to add incoming students and remove graduates or students who have left the program. The listserv should be used for official Grad Council communications, including meeting notices, agendas, minutes, elections, and opportunities for involvement.

#### 4.5 Vacancies

Board vacancies, anticipated or otherwise, shall be announced at a Grad Council meeting and communicated to the graduate student body via the listserv within seven (7) days. All eligible members must be given an equal opportunity to fill the position. The Board shall widely advertise any open position and may appoint an interim replacement within thirty (30) days of the vacancy. If more than one eligible member expresses interest, the Board may hold a special election consistent with the procedures in Article VII. If no replacement is identified within thirty (30) days, the Board may redistribute the duties of the vacant position among remaining Board Members until the position is filled or until the next annual election.

In the event that a Co-Chair is unable to fulfill their duties, the remaining Co-Chair shall serve in an interim capacity until the vacancy is filled. If both Co-Chairs are unable to fulfill their duties, the Finance and Operations Chair(s) shall convene the Board to appoint an interim presiding officer until a vacancy is filled.

#### 4.6 Accountability and Oversight

Board Members are responsible to the graduate student body and to one another for carrying out the mission of the Grad Council. A Board Member shall perform the duties of office, including any duties as a member of any Council committee on which they may serve, in good faith and in a manner such member believes to be in the best interests of the Grad Council, and with such care, including reasonable inquiry, as an ordinarily prudent person in a like situation would use under similar circumstances.

Non-performance:
The Co-Chairs will counsel Board Members who are not fulfilling the obligations and opportunities of their positions. In the instance that a Co-Chair has failed to perform their duties, the remaining Co-Chair (or, if needed, the Finance and Operations Chair(s)) will counsel the Co-Chair.

Grounds for removal from the Board include, but are not limited to:

- Preventing other Board Members from performing their appointed duties.
- Performing acts detrimental to the Grad Council, the Department of Sociology, or the graduate student body.
- Misuse of Grad Council funds or resources.
- Repeated unexcused absences, including absence from half of the regularly scheduled Executive Board meetings in a given academic quarter.

Any Board Member may request a removal vote by providing the Co-Chairs with a written statement of the grounds for removal. The vote shall occur at the next regularly scheduled Executive Board meeting (or at a special meeting called for this purpose). The Board Member in question shall be given notice and an opportunity to respond before the vote. Removal requires a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board Members present at a meeting with quorum. If a Board Member is removed or resigns, the resulting vacancy shall be filled pursuant to Section 4.5.

#### 5.2 Fall Institute

The Fall Institute (formerly the Spring Institute) is an annual sociology conference that is run by University of Chicago graduate students and held at the University of Chicago. The Planning Committee's responsibilities include:

1. Selecting a conference date and location as soon as possible.
2. Prioritizing the involvement of current University of Chicago PhD students in presenting their research to University of Chicago faculty.
3. Encouraging faculty to attend the conference to support current graduate students.
4. Inviting keynote and external speakers as needed.
5. Executing the planning of a successful conference day.
6. Working with the Finance and Operations team to determine a feasible budget for the conference.

### Article VI. Meetings

#### 6.1 All-Students Meetings

The Grad Council shall convene an All-Students Meeting at least once per academic quarter (Autumn, Winter, and Spring) and additionally as needed. When feasible, the All-Students Meeting will be scheduled to follow as closely as possible to a Department faculty meeting to discuss relevant matters.

All sociology graduate students may attend and participate. The All-Students Meeting serves as a space for the Executive Board to share updates, gather input, and discuss proposals and priorities with the graduate student body. Meetings may be held in-person, virtually, or in a hybrid format.

Meeting dates, times, and locations (or virtual links) will be announced at the beginning of each academic quarter and communicated to the graduate student body via the listserv at least seven (7) days in advance whenever possible. The Finance and Operations Chair(s) (or designee) will circulate an agenda in advance and make minutes available via email within one (1) week after the meeting.

#### 6.2 Executive Board Meetings

The Executive Board shall meet no less than monthly during the academic year and more frequently as needed. Executive Board meetings are open to any sociology graduate student. The Board may, by majority vote, move into closed session when discussing sensitive matters (for example, personal, confidential, or disciplinary issues).

The Co-Chairs are responsible for coordinating with Board Members to set a regular meeting time, establishing the agenda, and facilitating meetings. Should both Co-Chairs be absent, the authority to convene and facilitate the meeting moves to the Finance and Operations Chair(s), or another Board Member appointed by the Co-Chairs.

A majority of the current voting Board Members shall constitute a quorum. Motions require a quorum and pass with a strict majority of Board Members present.

### Article VII. Elections

The election process occurs in two stages: nomination, then voting. A nomination form will be sent to all students at least one month prior to the election dates. Students will then have the opportunity to accept or decline nominations, choosing only one if nominated for multiple board positions. Students can self-nominate or nominate others.

For Board positions with more people running than seats, candidates will write a two- to four-sentence blurb about what they would bring to the position.

Voting will occur online through an anonymous voting platform. The candidates with the most votes will be elected.

Elections will occur by the end of Spring Quarter (June), with nominations circulating at least one month prior (May).

### Article VIII. Finances

General:
The Grad Council has funds allocated by GSSAC and the Department to carry out its duties. This budget shall be managed by the Finance Chair with oversight from the Co-Chairs.

Dues:
The Grad Council shall not collect any dues from members.

### Article IX. Grad Council Communications

#### 9.1 Internal Communications

The Grad Council will maintain clear, timely, and accessible internal communications to promote transparency and engagement within the sociology graduate student body. Official communications shall be sent through the Grad Council listserv and/or other official channels maintained by the Council, and key documents will be archived in the Council's shared drive.

Meeting Minutes:
Minutes will be taken at All-Students Meetings and Executive Board meetings. Draft minutes should be circulated to the graduate student body within one (1) week after an All-Students Meeting and made available upon request after Executive Board meetings. Minutes will be stored in the Grad Council shared drive and, when appropriate, approved at the subsequent meeting.

Storing Student Information:
The Grad Council may maintain limited student contact information (for example, name, cohort year, University email) for the purposes of communicating with members and administering elections and events. Access to student information will be limited to current Board Members and handled in accordance with University policies. The Council should avoid storing sensitive personal information and should delete or update contact lists at least annually and during officer transitions.

Maintaining Records:
The Finance and Operations Chair(s) will maintain an organized, shared archive of Grad Council records, including agendas, minutes, budgets, funding applications, event planning documents, templates, and process notes. Financial records should be retained for a minimum of three (3) years (or longer if required by University policy). At the end of each academic year, the Board will ensure records are complete and transferred to incoming officers pursuant to Section 4.4.

#### 9.2 Interdepartmental Communications

The Grad Council will communicate and collaborate with other graduate student organizations and representative bodies within the University of Chicago (for example, departmental councils, division-level councils, and university-wide graduate student organizations) to share resources, coordinate programming, and advocate on issues affecting graduate students. When the Grad Council appoints a representative or liaison to an interdepartmental body, that representative will provide regular updates to the Executive Board and, as appropriate, to the graduate student body.

#### 9.3 Faculty and Staff Communications

The Grad Council Co-Chairs will manage communications between faculty Chairs, the DGS, and the Grad Council. The Co-Chairs are committed to transparency and will share important information with the student body in a timely manner. The Co-Chairs are also responsible for coordinating the quarterly Town Hall with the DGS and Department Chair to create an environment in which students can communicate directly with faculty leadership.

#### 9.4 Public Presence

Any public communications on behalf of the Sociology Grad Council will be discussed and approved by the Council in an All-Students Meeting.

### Article X. Amendments

Any member of the Grad Council may propose an amendment to the bylaws so long as the amendment is seconded by at least two other Grad Council members. The discussion will take place in an All-Students Meeting and voting will occur through an anonymized voting process following the meeting.
